Key Terms:

Thane - a member of an aristocratic class in Scotland.
Dagger Soliloquy - a speech Macbeth gives before committing regicide.
Witchcraft - the use of magic or sorcery, especially by adherents of Wicca.
Duncan - the King of Scotland.

Short Questions:

  1. Who is the King of Scotland in Act 1?
  2. What is Macbeth's initial reaction to the witches' prophecies?
  3. What does Lady Macbeth mean when she says "look like th' innocent flower, but be the serpent under 't"?
  4. What does Banquo suspect about Macbeth?

Quotation Analysis:

Read the following quote from Macbeth Act 1 Scene 7 and answer the question below:

"I have no spur to prick the sides of my intent, but only vaulting ambition, which o'erleaps itself and falls on the other."

What does Macbeth mean by "vaulting ambition"?

Open-Ended Research:

Research and discuss the historical significance of the play Macbeth in the context of Shakespeare's works. How does the play reflect the societal norms and beliefs of the time period in which it was written?
