The Formation of Volcanoes

Volcanoes are geological features that have fascinated humans for centuries. They are formed when molten rock, ash, and gas escape from the Earth's crust. Here are some key terms to help you understand the formation of volcanoes:

  • Magma - molten rock beneath the earth's surface
  • Lava - molten rock that has reached the earth's surface
  • Volcanic ash - tiny particles of rock, minerals, and glass
  • Pyroclastic flow - a fast-moving current of hot gas and rock that can reach speeds of up to 450 miles per hour
