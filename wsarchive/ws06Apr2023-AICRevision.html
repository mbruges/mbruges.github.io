Answers to Sections 1 and 2

1. Who is the author of the play?

Answer: J.B Priestley.

2. What is the name of the family in the play?

Answer: The Birlings.

3. What is the name of the inspector who visits the family?

Answer: Inspector Goole.

4. What is the main event that takes place before the start of the play?

Answer: Eva Smith/Daisy Renton's suicide.

5. Who is the character responsible for this event?

Answer: Each member of the Birling family, along with Gerald Croft, played a role in Eva Smith/Daisy Renton's suicide.

1. Describe the character of Sheila Birling.

Answer: Sheila is a young woman who begins the play as a somewhat superficial and privileged girl, but who ultimately becomes remorseful and empathetic after learning about her role in Eva Smith/Daisy Renton's death.

2. What is Mr. Birling's profession and how does it affect his personality?

Answer: Mr. Birling is a wealthy businessman who values money and status above all else. His profession makes him arrogant, selfish, and dismissive of anyone he deems beneath him.

3. What is the role of Gerald Croft in the play?

Answer: Gerald is Sheila's fianc√© and a member of an aristocratic family. He plays a central role in Eva Smith/Daisy Renton's story, as he had an affair with her and ultimately contributed to her downfall.

4. How does Eric Birling's behavior change throughout the play?

Answer: At the beginning of the play, Eric is a heavy drinker who seems to have little direction in life. However, as the story progresses and he learns about his role in Eva Smith/Daisy Renton's death, he becomes more introspective and begins to take responsibility for his actions.

5. Describe the personality of Inspector Goole.

Answer: Inspector Goole is a mysterious and imposing figure who seems to know everything about the characters in the play. He is persistent, unemotional, and unyielding in his pursuit of the truth, and seems to represent a moral force or a higher power.
