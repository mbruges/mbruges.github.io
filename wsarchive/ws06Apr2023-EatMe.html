Comprehension

Answer the following questions about the poem:

  1. What gift does the man give the speaker on her 30th birthday? Why is this ironic?
  2. How does the man's language show his objectification of the speaker?
  3. Why does the speaker say "I was his Jacuzzi"? What metaphor is being used here?
  4. How does the ending subvert the reader's expectations?

Language analysis

Discuss the effectiveness of the following language features in the poem:

  • The repetition of "eat me"
  • The use of similes and metaphors to describe the speaker, e.g. "beached whale", "tidal wave of flesh"
  • The increasing length of lines, reflecting the speaker's increasing size
  • The casual, imperative tone of the man's language

Discussion

The poem explores themes of objectification, abuse and control. Discuss how these themes are conveyed through the metaphor of eating and the speaker's increasing size.

You could consider:

  • The man's language in coercing the speaker to eat and gain weight
  • The pleasure he gains from watching her size increase
  • The ways in which the speaker's expanding body represents her loss of control and independence
  • How the ending suggests the speaker regaining power over the man
