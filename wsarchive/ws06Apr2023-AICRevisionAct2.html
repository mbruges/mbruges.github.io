Revision Worksheet for 'An Inspector Calls' - Part Two

Section 1: Comprehension and Recall

  1. What is the setting of Act Two?
  2. Who is the first person to realize that Eric is the father of Eva/Daisy's child?
  3. What does Mrs. Birling suggest Eva/Daisy should have done when she came to the Women's Charity Organization?
  4. What does Sheila do when she realizes that her engagement with Gerald is over?
  5. What does Mr. Birling do when he realizes that Eric stole money to support Eva/Daisy?
  6. What is the Inspector's reaction to Eric's confession?
  7. What is the final piece of information that the Inspector reveals before leaving the stage?
  8. What is the mood or tone at the departure of the Inspector?
Click to reveal answers
  1. The setting of Act Two is the Birling family's dining room.
  2. The first person to realize that Eric is the father of Eva/Daisy's child is Sheila.
  3. When Eva/Daisy came to the Women's Charity Organization, Mrs. Birling suggested that she should have gone to the father of her child for help.
  4. When Sheila realizes that her engagement with Gerald is over, she accepts the situation calmly and maturely.
  5. When Mr. Birling realizes that Eric stole money to support Eva/Daisy, he tries to cover up the situation and protect his reputation.
  6. The Inspector's reaction to Eric's confession is shock and amazement.
  7. The final piece of information that the Inspector reveals before leaving at is that the Birlings' actions have consequences beyond their own lives.
  8. The mood or tone is pessimistic and despairing.

Section 2: Analysis and Interpretation

Choose one of the following prompts and write a paragraph or two in response:

  1. Discuss the significance of the Birling family's reactions to Eric's confession in Act Two.
  2. Explain how the theme of class is developed in Act Two, using specific examples from the text.
  3. Compare and contrast Sheila's character development in Act Two with that of Gerald's. How are they similar or different?
  4. Analyze the Inspector's role in Act Two. What is his purpose and how does he affect the other characters?

Section 3: Creative Response

Choose one of the following prompts and respond in a creative way (e.g. a short story, a poem, a drawing, a video, etc.):

  1. Write a continuation of the play, imagining what might happen next in the story.
  2. Create a character sketch of one of the characters, using descriptive language and specific examples from the text.
  3. Design a set or costume for a production of 'An Inspector Calls', focusing on the themes and motifs of Act Two.
  4. Compose a song or rap that summarizes the events and themes of Act Two in 'An Inspector Calls'.

Section 4: Extension and Research

Choose one of the following prompts and conduct some independent research to respond:

  1. Research the historical and social context of 'An Inspector Calls', focusing on the time period in which it is set (1912) and written (1945). What were some of the major political, economic, and social issues of this time?
  2. Explore the concept of socialism as it is presented in 'An Inspector Calls'. How does the play critique capitalist ideology and promote socialist ideals?
  3. Investigate the life and works of J B Priestley, the playwright of 'An Inspector Calls'. What were some of his influences, themes, and techniques as a writer?
  4. Compare and contrast 'An Inspector Calls' with another play or text that explores similar themes or issues (e.g. 'Death of a Salesman', 'A Doll's House', '1984', etc.). How are they similar or different in terms of style, structure, characters, and themes?
