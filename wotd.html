The Word of The Day is SYNTHESIS 📚

Synthesis refers to the process of combining different elements, ideas or concepts to create a new whole.

The word synthesis comes from the Greek words ‘sun’ and ‘thesis,’ where ‘sun’ means ‘together’ and ‘thesis’ means ‘placing, positioning or arrangement.’

Example Sentences:

  1. She used synthesis to combine the ideas from her various academic papers into one coherent thesis.
  2. Through the synthesis of the findings from different studies, we were able to draw new conclusions.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Write down four different ideas or concepts and try to find a way to synthesize them into a new whole.
  2. Practice your synthesis skills by creating a mind map or diagram that shows the different elements you would need to combine to create your ideal job.
  3. Choose two opposing views on a controversial topic and try to synthesize them into a more nuanced, balanced view.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story where the main character has to use synthesis to overcome a complex problem.

Drawing Task:

Draw a picture that represents synthesis. This could involve combining different shapes, colours or images to create a new whole.
