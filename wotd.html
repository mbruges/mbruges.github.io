The Word of The Day is HYPERBOLE 💡

Hyperbole: exaggerated statements or claims not meant to be taken literally.

Etymology: from Greek huperbolƒì, meaning "excess".

Example Sentences:

1. I have told you a million times to clean your room. (Hyperbole used to emphasize the number of times the speaker has made the statement)

2. He's running faster than the speed of light. (Hyperbole used to emphasize the extreme speed of the runner)

Practice Tasks:

  1. Identify the hyperbole in the following sentence: I am so hungry, I could eat a horse.
  2. Which of the following is an example of hyperbole?
    a. The sun is really hot today.
    b. I have a million things to do.
    c. She is as sweet as sugar.
  3. Use hyperbole to describe how excited you are for the weekend.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story that uses at least three examples of hyperbole. Be creative and have fun!

Drawing Task:

Create a drawing that includes at least one example of hyperbole. This could be a cartoon or a scene from your story.
